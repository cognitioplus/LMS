Course Deep Dive

I. Course Overview: Purpose, Frameworks, and Philosophy

Purpose: This certification course equips mental health and well-being paraprofessionals with the essential knowledge, values, and practical skills to provide safe, ethical, and compassionate peer-based psychosocial support online and in community contexts.

Guiding Frameworks

  • WHO Mental Health Gap Action Programme (mhGAP), 2023 update
  • WHO QualityRights Initiative: Dignity, Recovery, and Human Rights in Mental Health
  • Philippine Council for Mental Health (PCMH) Strategic Framework 2024–2028:
    • Pillar 1: Service Delivery and Workforce Development
    • Pillar 2: Mental Health Promotion and Prevention
    • Pillar 3: Governance, Data, and Research
    • Pillar 4: Cross-Sectoral Collaboration

Course Philosophy: The course applies Psychological First Aid (PFA) principles—Look, Listen, Link—and builds foundational competencies in empathy, communication, and referral, ensuring participants can respond to mental health needs safely within their scope.

III. Course Learning Outcomes

Upon completion, trainees will be able to:

  1. Apply generative listening, empathy, and rapport-building in online or face-to-face conversations.
  2. Demonstrate ethical communication, confidentiality, and data privacy in online interactions.
  3. Recognize and respond appropriately to psychological distress and crisis indicators.
  4. Identify when and how to refer individuals to professional mental health or community services.
  5. Apply self-care, boundaries, and mindfulness practices to sustain personal resilience.
  6. Uphold human rights, cultural sensitivity, and inclusivity in all interactions.

V. Course Modules (Foundational Training)

Module 0: Introduction and Orientation

Duration: 1 hour

Objectives:

  • Orient learners to course structure, purpose, and ethical commitments.
  • Introduce the role of the paraprofessional within the mental health ecosystem.
Go to Module 0

Module 1: Foundations of Mental Health and Well-Being

Duration: 3 hours

Learning Outcomes:

  • Define mental health, mental illness, and psychosocial well-being.
  • Describe key components of the Philippine Mental Health Act (RA 11036).
  • Explain community and online support within the mental health continuum.
Go to Module 1

Module 2: Ethics, Boundaries, and Digital Confidentiality

Duration: 2 hours

Learning Outcomes:

  • Demonstrate respect for confidentiality and privacy.
  • Identify ethical risks in online peer support.
Go to Module 2

Module 3: Psychological First Aid (Look–Listen–Link)

Duration: 3 hours

Learning Outcomes:

  • Apply PFA principles to emotional and mental distress.
  • Recognize common reactions to crisis or trauma.
Go to Module 3

Module 4: Core Support and Basic Coping Skills

Duration: 6 hours

Learning Outcomes:

  • Demonstrate eight core support and communication skills.
  • Demonstrate skill in sharing basic coping skills.
Go to Module 4

Module 5: Recognizing and Responding to Crisis

Duration: 4 hours

Learning Outcomes:

  • Identify warning signs of suicide or acute distress.
  • Demonstrate appropriate response and referral steps.
Go to Module 5

Module 6: Cultural Sensitivity and Community Context

Duration: 2 hours

Learning Outcomes:

  • Adapt communication and support to diverse cultural and linguistic settings.
  • Recognize indigenous, faith-based, and family-centered approaches.
Go to Module 6

Module 7: Self-Care and Resilience

Duration: 3 hours

Learning Outcomes:

  • Recognize signs of burnout and compassion fatigue.
  • Develop a personalized self-care plan and mindfulness routine.
Go to Module 7

Module 8: Practicum and Mentorship (Part 2)

Duration: 16 hours (field + supervision)

Objectives:

  • Apply all eight core support skills in supervised online or community sessions.
  • Receive mentor feedback on ethics, empathy, and communication.
Go to Module 8

Module 9: Integration and Certification

Duration: 1 hour

Activities:

  • Post-test (minimum passing score 80%)
  • Feedback survey and self-assessment
Go to Module 9
